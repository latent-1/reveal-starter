Qualifying Explained

Q1 - First Stage

20 minutes • Bottom 5 eliminated

Sets positions 16-20

Q2 - Second Stage

15 minutes • Next 5 eliminated

Sets positions 11-15

Q3 - Final Stage

12 minutes • Top 10 battle

Sets positions 1-10

🥇 Pole Position

The fastest qualifier starts from the front of the grid

A good qualifying position is crucial for race success

Flag System

🟢 Green Flag

Race start or restart after caution period

🟡 Yellow Flag

Caution - slow down, no overtaking

🔴 Red Flag

Race stopped - return to pit lane

🔵 Blue Flag

Lapped car must let faster car pass

🏁 Checkered Flag

End of race - Victory!

Tire Strategy

🔴 Soft Tires

Fastest lap times

Wear out quickly

🟡 Medium Tires

Balanced performance

Moderate wear rate

⚪ Hard Tires

Long-lasting

Slower lap times

⛽ Pit Stop Strategy

Mandatory tire changes during the race

Teams must use at least two different tire compounds

Tire degradation affects speed and handling

Penalties and Regulations

⏰ Time Penalties

5 or 10-second penalties added to race time

🔄 Drive-Through

Must drive through pit lane at speed limit

📍 Grid Penalties

Starting position moved back on the grid

⚖️ FIA Enforcement

International body governing F1 regulations

Common infractions include unsafe releases, track limits, and dangerous driving
